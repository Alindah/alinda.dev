Who am I?
My name is Alinda! I am a software engineer living in San Francisco.
My Origin Story
A Los Angeles native, I found myself in NorCal after San Francisco's beckoning in 2019. I graduated from CSU Channel Islands in 2017 with a B.S. in Computer Science, with a sprinkle of Mathematics and dash of Robotics Engineering.

"What do you want to be when you grow up?" has always been a challenging question to me. My education points me to tech; however, my interests aren't limited to practical pursuits. In fact, my career ambitions began on the creative side. I wanted to be a painter, an architect, a tattoo artist... and then later a surgeon, a video game designer, and a paramedic.

I realized I couldn't just pick one thing when there are so many interesting roles to play! My dream is to become a polymath — someone who excels in many disciplines. Hey, this makes me an excellent generalist!

It wasn't until my second year of college that I discovered programming. I took a Visual Basic intro class on a whim, and found that programming is a good balance between left brain and right brain; it's a practical skill that allows me to create. By the end of the year, I switched my major from biology to computer science. And the rest is history.
What Am I Doing These Days?
Besides spending my days hiding out from COVID, I've been working on personal projects and watching my brainchildren come to life!

I mainly satisfy my thirst for creation by making digital art. More recently, I learned how to make my own music using a DAW. Someday, I'm going to make my very own bona fide video games.

My other hobbies involve trekking through the digital world of video games (FPS and RPGs are my favorite!) and playing guitar. I might also be pretending to be a little boy in a D&D campaign. Otherwise, you can find me in the kitchen where I cook really bland foods (but let's not talk about that).

Anyway, I pay $12 / year for this site and have poured hours into it, so I hope you enjoy your visit!

i-Ready: Cloud Machine
Tools

C#, Unity
Background

I worked on this game during my time on the i-Ready Learning Games team at Curriculum Associates. Cloud Machine is a game that teaches fractional concepts through imagery.

My main work was in prototyping the game and its mobile features. I also worked on the UI functionality, Spanish localization implementation, and fixing various bugs throughout the production.

This was one of my favorite projects to work on. It was exciting to watch this project from its birth during office brainstorming to its release to over 150,000 students in its first week!

i-Ready: Cupcake
Tools

C#, Unity
Background

Another game I worked on while on the i-Ready Learning Games team at Curriculum Associates. Cupcake is a simulation game where students manage a cupcake store. They practice solving arithmetic problems, analyzing word problems, and reading coordinates as they buy ingredients, take orders, and deliver cupcakes to quirky customers.

My focus was in fixing bugs, fixing layering issues, and adding Spanish localization. ¡Aprendí español un poquito porque trabajé en este juego!

Flower Frenzy
Tools

C#, Unity
Background

Chomper the gardener is in charge of babysitting the demanding little lamb, Eleanor. But boy, is she picky! She will only eat the exact number of flowers she demands. Luckily, Chomper is a patient T. rex who eagerly takes up this daunting task.

Flower Frenzy is a simple math game I made before joining Curriculum Associates. Its goal is to teach players multiplication by requiring them to figure out what combination of numbers can be multiplied together to satisfy Eleanor's demand.

Fun fact: Chomper and Eleanor are modeled after my stuffed animals. :)

Danny & Rosses
Overview

Genre - Retro
Duration - 1:40
Tools

Reaper DAW, Sforzando VST
Background

When I was a kid, I wanted to make a movie with my sisters, but no one wanted to play with me. Not one to be dissuaded, I grabbed my trusty camcorder and filmed a first-person POV video of my scrappy tiger character, Timmy, going on an adventure. I improvised an "on-a-mission" theme song to accompany his shenanigans, and this song here was largely inspired by that childhood piece.

While I've recorded many song ideas in the past, this is my very first completed song. I'm proud of it! I plan to use it for a recreation of a childhood video game idea featuring my two characters, Danny and Rosses, thus I repurposed it to have a more retro video game feel compared to its original concept.
