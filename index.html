Who am I?
My name is Alinda! I am a software engineer living in San Francisco.
My Origin Story
Born and raised in Los Angeles, I'd lived in the City of Angels for all of my life until San Francisco's beckoning in 2019. I graduated from CSU Channel Islands in 2017 with a B.S. in Computer Science, with a sprinkle of Mathematics and a dash of Robotics Engineering.

"What do you want to be when you grow up?" has always been a challenging question to me. My education points me to tech; however, my interests aren't limited to practical pursuits. In fact, my career ambitions began on the creative side. I wanted to be a painter, an architect, a tattoo artist... and then later a surgeon, a video game designer, and a paramedic.

I realized I couldn't just pick one thing when there are so many interesting roles to play! My dream is to become a Renaissance woman (otherwise known as a polymath) — someone who excels in many disciplines. Hey, this makes me an excellent generalist!

It wasn't until my second year of college that I discovered programming. I took a Visual Basic intro class on a whim, and found that programming is a good balance between left brain and right brain; it's a practical skill that allows me to create. By the end of the year, I switched my major from biology to computer science. And the rest is history.
What Am I Doing These Days?
Besides spending my days hiding out from COVID, I've been working on personal projects and watching my brainchildren come to life!

I mainly satisfy my thirst for creation by making digital art. More recently, I learned how to make my own music using a DAW. Someday, I'm going to make my very own bona fide video games.

My other hobbies involve adventuring through the digital world of video games (FPS and RPGs are my favorite!) and playing guitar. I might also be pretending to be a little boy in a D&D campaign. Otherwise, you can find me in the kitchen where I cook really bland foods (but let's not talk about that).

Anyway, I pay $12 / year for this site and have poured hours into it, so I hope you enjoy your visit!
